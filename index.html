Meet Gisèle

I was born in Co. Kerry to parents who shared an abiding faith in the possibilities of education. My experience studying, graduating and working with and for Trinity postgraduates, shaped my ‘I CARE’ philosophy. It has allowed me to collaborate whilst always being independent, and I will remain so, if elected Senator. I will continue to lobby for more investment in innovation, inclusion and safety, more affordable housing and better public services; more participation and inclusion; more resources for research and for the arts; more environmental/human rights. I want to bring care into Seanad decision making.

In a changing national and global landscape, I believe we need a collective vision for society, economy and the environment. If elected I will draw on my wide experience in Trinity during my 20,000 plus hours worked as an elected representative and President of the Graduate Students’ Union plus my former career at national and international level to serve as a clear, courageous and effective independent voice to build with you in Seanad Éireann.

Campaigning has helped me debate important public issues

I have a positive record at coalition building and lobbying

I have a long history of advocacy and activism in climate justice and human rights

In my most recent campaigning, I am challenging the Higher Education Authority Bill as it endangers Trinity’s autonomy and am championing the reopening of the Science Gallery.

I launched a petition to save College Park in October 2021 from being built on which was signed by thousands of students and staff. The campaign was ultimately successful and plans to build on College Park were scrapped by Trinity in February 2022.

I lobbied on behalf of environmental causes, namely co-managing the protection of a red-listed bird breed in Trinity College Dublin during the pandemic.

I led the Union in challenging the marketing and selling of unsustainable foods to students on campus and backed the immediate divestment from fossil fuels and arms in Trinity.

My memberships and partnerships include: Amnesty International 1961, Birdwatch Ireland, the Irish Georgian Society, Irish Wildlife Trust, Trinity Women Graduates 1922 centenary committee member, College Historical Society, The Hist (1747), University Philosophical Society - The Phil, (1683) and the International Academy of Digital Arts and Sciences (rewards international advocacy online and online safety).

In October 2021, I co-led postgraduates in a protest to Dáil Éireann criticising the provisions for third level in budget 2022. I protested the government on the lack of mental health resources at Trinity College Dublin.

In November 2021, I co-wrote an open-letter seeking urgent intervention to protect the mental health of students scheduled to sit in-person exams during the pandemic and delivered it with 5000 signatures in December 2021 to the Minister for Further and Higher Education, Research, Innovation and Science.

September 2020, I partnered with the Forum for Disabled Staff and Postgraduate Students in Trinity College Dublin to appoint a Disability officer from the Forum membership to the Graduate Students’ Union executive.

I introduced Student Parent Officer’s role to the Graduate Students’ Union to improve pregnancy supports and funding for student parents.

I lobbied and secured the development of a new occasional staff and casual pay policy at Trinity Finance Committee in June 2021, This benefits demonstrators in labs, teaching assistants and administrative and technical workers.

In April 2021, dissatisfied with the rate of pay stimulus to postgraduates I lobbied to ensure that those employed directly or indirectly with the College are at least furnished with a living wage.

On June 5, 2021 I successfully got the living wage paid to students working in the Trinity Business School.

I co-launched a petition in conjunction with Ukrainian students to call on the IFSC to place sanctions on the 34 Billion in Russian money in different vehicles which needs to be scrutinised. We need to ensure that Ireland isn’t used for Russian oligarchs to launder money.

Secured a GSU mandate to explore Seanad reform and to continue to lobby for full democracy for all citizens.

My Priorities

My priorities if elected will include:

Innovation
More Investment in Innovation, Inclusion and Safety

I worked tirelessly to support safety and security in Trinity during COVID-19 and have been a strong advocate for measures to challenge violence against others, including management of a safe Library and study space programme for the Trinity College community.

I will continue to advocate for safe space and resources to tackle violence at a national level, and I will also promote innovative safety policies online and in the digital sphere. I want to support and inform citizens on how to protect their data and to demand their digital rights and policy for safety in the workplace, at home, online and in the wider community.

As a member of Trinity’s Tangent Academic Oversight Committee, I have seen how critical it is to press for more investment in innovative third-level life-long learning and accessibility programmes so that everyone can be a participant at a national level in building a safer, inclusive, successful Ireland.

A real recovery needs to build from the base up by investing in public services such as health, education and social housing. We need to ensure there is access to decent work for decent pay wherever you live in the country so that money flows back into local shops and services. There is, I believe, also an important role for cultural participation and community development as the seedbeds of creativity and innovation.

Community
More Housing and Public Services

At the Harvard Kennedy School of Government, I developed new housing policy in 2021 with the mayor’s office in Burlington, Vermont, to compliment Bernie Sander’s policy and bring equality in housing.

I’ve pushed for public housing and rental protection, raised money for the homeless, and published academically on homelessness. Currently there are over 9000 experiencing homelessness and a mere several hundred properties available to them in the private rental market. This needs to change.

We deserve a healthy housing system where families can access proper homes through both the market and social housing. We need a new national long-term building programme to create more homes.

I believe we can create innovative solutions and deliver quality public services as a national priority. I will support a rights-based approach to health, housing, affordable childcare and education and legislate to ensure equality of access.

Continue to advocate passionately for serious resources to tackle violence against citizens, greater investment in childcare and early years and the closing of the gender pension gap.

Having worked closely with both older people and with young people in unemployment, I also recognise the importance of adequate resourcing for youth work, social care and the mental health supports so essential to the full participation of people of all ages.

I want to see long term thinking and collaborative community engagement in our policy-making. This means recognising care as the glue of our society and reflecting that in our economic policies. We also need to invest in affordable, accessible childcare, plan for people in our community who need wraparound mental health support or experience a physical or hidden disability at some point in their lives and ensure that the priceless contribution made by family carers is properly valued and supported.

Accessibility
More Participation and Inclusion

I stand for a culture and society where everyone can participate fully. From helping those with hidden disabilities in our community through the Trinity College Disability Service to delivering notetaking and facilitation supports around exams and lectures, I always take real and direct action to support inclusion.

I work daily with young people who lose employment or shelter and I understand at grassroots level the impediments faced and the investment needed in education, community development, social care and mental health supports.

I will continue to promote equality of representation in all areas of national life, including arts, sport, STEM and our educational institutions and I am committed to community engagement to help bring Seanad reform.

The Seanad does have a track record in delivering fair debate and good legislation, but it urgently needs to be reformed. As a national house, it should represent the full nation. If elected, I will be fighting for a vote for all citizens, building on the proposals put forward by former and current senators. I am disappointed that our Government, after its failed attempt to abolish the Seanad, has done so little to improve our political institutions. Political reform will be a key priority for me in the coming years.

Research & The Arts
More Resources for Research & the Arts

At Trinity, I secured contracts for PhD workers, advocate for institutional autonomy and continue to call for a reformed pay policy for research in STEAM, Humanities and the Arts.

Pressing government to properly resource mental health supports, cancer care and the National Dementia Strategy will be a priority.

Medical, nursing and social care graduates and teachers need to be valued, cared for and encouraged to stay in Ireland. I’ll be fighting to improve their working conditions, so we can value them in our system.

As an author, I understand the power and value of culture and I will work tirelessly to bring in new policies that protect and support the rights and pay of writers, poets, musicians and auxiliary technicians within the Arts landscape.

I will continue to pursue funders and Government for investment in research. Without adequate funding for both our education and higher education sectors, our services, quality of research, and results and inevitably our rankings and reputations will slide. I will advocate for greater funding and investment in our services and the lifeblood of the education sector which is our researchers, our academics and our teachers countrywide.

I will continue to challenge the Higher Education Authority Bill to protect the universities autonomy. Protecting our academic freedom against government intervention is imperative and I will continue to challenge and push back at the government strongly on this as a progressive, independent voice in the Seanad, devoid of any party.

Equality
More Environmental/Human Rights

I have a strong track record on environmental issues and climate justice for over a decade, campaigning for an exit from fossil fuels and worked hard to protect pollinators and biodiversity.

I activated the swift bird callers over two years during the pandemic to save a red listed swift bird species on the Trinity campus and helped secure safe bicycle corridors in Trinity and the wider city.

I lead students and staff in the campaign to #HelpSaveCollegePark. We are in the midst of three environmental emergencies: climate change, biodiversity collapse and pollution. I will address all three headon and promote a Just Transition to a cleaner, greener Ireland.

In my work I have recognised and supported the diverse identities and experiences of our graduates and advocated for the rights of minorities, the marginalised and the LGBTQ+I community who suffer persecution worldwide. I will promote equality of representation in all areas of national life including, sport, STEM, our educational institutions and the arts. I will also continue to speak up and show solidarity on international human rights.

I welcome the government’s Climate Action Plan to reduce Ireland’s greenhouse gas emission by 51% by 2030. However, efforts are currently backloaded towards the end of the decade, and I believe instead we should be prioritising the reduction of emissions earlier.

Climate action must be just, and take into account the needs of ordinary people. The burden must be shared out fairly. This is why I advocate for a Just Transition to a cleaner, greener Ireland.

In the Global South, people who have historically had the least contribution to environmental degradation are experiencing the very worst of climate change. This needs to be addressed. I am therefore advocating for Climate Justice.

Our waterways are polluted and our list of threatened species is growing. I have worked hard to protect now red-listed swifts in Trinity College - but we need to do more to protect our beleaguered wildlife across the country. Therefore I’ll be asking the government to create solid targets as a matter of urgency.

A message from Gisèle Scanlon

Teachtaireacht ó Gisèle Ní Scannláin

Dear fellow Trinity graduate,

I am writing to ask for your first preference vote at the upcoming Seanad Bye-Election.

It has been a privilege to serve as a strong representative voice within Trinity College over the last five years, from student officer through to President of the Graduate Students’ Union. As a fearless independent voice with a track record on equality and inclusion, I have always fought to protect the most vulnerable, both within Trinity and beyond its gates.

I campaigned vigorously for many years on mental health care for students, staff, schoolchildren and people fleeing persecution. Throughout the recent pandemic, I was on the ground offering practical and principled support to students and workers on issues from exams safety to improvements in rental security and employment conditions. If elected, I will continue to champion crucial issues like quality employment, affordable housing and childcare for everyone in society.

My work with and for postgraduates across diverse disciplines and backgrounds has not only deepened my insight of the issues Ireland faces, it has also given me insight into solutions. I seek accountability, value for money, practical improvements and proper recognition for our educators and caregivers.

As a champion of both heritage and innovation, I was a leader in the successful campaign to Save College Park and in ongoing efforts to save the Science Gallery. I worked to support the saving of red listed swifts and have pushed for stronger environmental and climate action in Trinity and wider Dublin, where I have pushed for greater investment in cycling infrastructure and the protection of our urban tree canopy.

I also played an active role in national campaigns like campaigning for the Irish language’s official status, Repeal the 8th and Yes Equality.

Having left a successful career as an author to return to academic studies, my time and work in Trinity College Dublin shaped me and inspired me to dedicate myself to public service.

If you elect me to the Seanad, I will be a strong voice for you, for Trinity and for society.

I hope you will consider that I deserve your support. Please encourage friends and family to give me your No.1. Vote or highest possible preference.

Le mile buíochas agus gach dea-ghuí,
Yours sincerely,
Gisèle
